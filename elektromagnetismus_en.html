🧲 Magnetism Adventure ⚡

1. Der Strom macht den Magneten

Did you know that electricity has a superpower? As soon as current flows through a cable, it builds up an invisible force around the cable. We call this a magnetic field.

📖 The Smart Word: Magnetic Field
A magnetic field is like an invisible "cloud" of force. Anything magnetic that enters this cloud is attracted or repelled. We often draw this force as rings called field lines.

2. The Magic Coil & the Super Iron

A straight wire is only a weak magnet. But if we wind the wire like a spring, we call it a coil. The forces of each turn then work together!

📖 The Smart Word: Ferromagnetism
That sounds complicated, but it just means: some materials (like iron) can be magnetized extremely easily. They help the coil bundle its force and become much stronger.

3. Lorentz Force: The Magnetic Push

When electricity and magnetism meet, a force is created that pushes things away. We call this push Lorentz force. That's why motors spin!

📖 The Smart Word: Electric Motor
A motor transforms electricity into motion. Through the magnetic "push" (Lorentz force), a wheel inside is constantly nudged until it spins very fast.

4. Induction: DIY Electricity

It also works the other way around: if you move a magnet very fast inside a coil, the magnetic field "pushes" the electrons. This creates electricity from pure motion! This is called induction.

📖 The Smart Word: Generator
A generator does the opposite of a motor: it makes electricity from motion (e.g., from wind). It's like a small power factory.
