Light always travels exactly straight. If you draw the outermost light rays (edge rays) past an obstacle, you can calculate to the millimeter exactly where a shadow falls!

2. The Solar Eclipse (Construction)

The small moon pushes itself in front of the huge sun. If we draw the light rays from the top and bottom edge of the sun past the moon, we see two areas: the deep black umbra (core shadow) and the gray penumbra (partial shadow).

Where not a single sun ray gets to anymore, the eclipse is total (umbra).

Why is it suddenly dark during a total solar eclipse?

3. The Lunar Eclipse (Construction)

The earth is much larger than the moon. The straight edge rays of the sun form a huge, long umbra tube behind the earth. The moon fits completely into it!

The moon dives completely into the deep shadow of the earth.
