1. Licht wandert geradeaus

Light is extremely fast (almost 300,000 kilometers per second!). But the most important thing is: light rays are like super-straight rulers. They always fly on the direct path as long as nothing stands in their way.

📖 The Smart Word: Light Source
Everything that shines by itself (Sun, flashlight, fireflies) is called a light source. We only see other things because they reflect (bounce back) light.

Light rays spread out in a straight line in all directions.

2. Reflection: The Mirror Trick

When light hits a very smooth surface (like a mirror or still water), it bounces off like a ball against a wall. We call this reflection.

📖 Angle of Incidence = Angle of Reflection
This is the most important law: light arrives at a certain angle and is sent back at exactly the same angle.

3. Lichtbrechung: Der Knick-Effekt

Have you ever noticed that a straw in a glass of water looks "broken"? This is because light travels slower in water than in air. When passing through, the light makes a little bend!

Refraction makes objects under water appear displaced.
