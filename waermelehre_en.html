1. What is Heat? The Big Shiver!

Whether water, wood, or air – everything around us consists of tiny little building blocks, the particles. And these particles never sit still! They wobble and shiver all the time. Heat is basically nothing other than movement! The hotter something is, the more wildly and faster the particles race around. When it gets cold, they move very slowly.

📖 The Smart Dictionary: Absolute Zero
Is there a point where the particles freeze completely and don't move at all? Yes! That's called "absolute zero". It lies at a freezing -273.15 degrees Celsius. It doesn't get colder than that in the entire universe!

2. Thermal Conduction: The Fast Spoon Rally

When you stir a hot cocoa, the spoon eventually gets warm at the handle. Why? The fast, hot particles in the cocoa push the particles at the bottom of the spoon. These push their neighbors, and so the "wobble energy" travels up the spoon. This is called thermal conduction.

📖 The Smart Dictionary: Conductors and Insulators
- Good thermal conductors: Metals (like copper or iron) pass heat on extremely quickly. That's why cooking pots are made of metal!
- Poor thermal conductors (insulators): Wood, plastic, or air hardly let heat through. That's why a winter jacket has many thick air cushions – so your body heat doesn't escape!

3. Thermal Radiation: Heat Without Touch

Remember the vacuum in space (without air particles)? How does the heat of the sun reach us on earth if there is nothing there that could "conduct" the heat? The answer: thermal radiation (infrared light)! It travels like light extremely fast through empty space and warms us up as soon as it hits our skin.

📖 Colors Make the Difference: Black vs. White
Dark things (like a black T-shirt) "swallow" the thermal radiation (absorption) and quickly become extremely hot. Light-colored things (like white snow) throw the rays back like a mirror (reflection) and stay cooler.

4. Thermal Convection: Why Hot Air Can Fly

Hot particles move faster and need more space. This causes hot air to expand and become "lighter" than the cold air around it. What happens? The hot air rises! This is called thermal convection (or heat current). Cold air sinks downwards in exchange.

📖 The Hot Air Balloon Secret
A hot air balloon has no engine! The pilot just fires a huge gas burner. The air in the huge envelope becomes hot, expands and pulls the basket into the sky as if by magic. If you let the air cool down, the balloon sinks again.

5. States of Matter: Ice, Water, Steam!

Heat can completely transform things! Almost every substance has three forms of appearance (states of matter): solid, liquid, and gaseous. This is again due to our particles! If they are very cold, they cuddle closely together and are rigid (ice). If it gets warmer, they loosen up and flow around (water). If it gets really hot, they fly wildly apart into the air (water vapor).

📖 The Magic Words of Physics:
- Solid to liquid = Melting (snowman in the sun)
- Liquid to gaseous = Vaporizing (boiling pasta water)
- Gaseous to liquid = Condensing (mirror fogs up while showering)

6. Thermal Expansion: Everything spreads out!

Because the particles wobble faster when heated, they need more space to "dance". The result: Objects expand when they get hot! This happens with solid things, with liquids, and with gases. When it gets cold, they contract again.

📖 The Thermometer Secret
Old thermometers use exactly this trick. At the bottom is a red liquid (often alcohol). If it gets warm outside, the red liquid expands and rises in the thin glass tube. We read the temperature!
